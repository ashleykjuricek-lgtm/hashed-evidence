## What Was Found

A computation on the Penrose tiling (quasiperiodic lattice generated by cut-and-project from Z⁵→R²) reveals that the ratio of phason-shifted to unshifted lattice zeta sums gives **1/φ** (the golden ratio reciprocal) at exponent **s = 2**, when the phason shift is directed along a vertex direction of the decagonal acceptance window.

This is the quasiperiodic analog of the known periodic result: on the three-torus T³, the ratio of antiperiodic to periodic Epstein zeta gives **1/24**.

### Key Results

- **Ratio = 1/φ at s ≈ 2.0** — converges monotonically as lattice size N → ∞
- **Direction-dependent:** ratio hits 1/φ along exactly 10 directions (decagonal vertices, every 36°), drops to ~0.522 between
- **Shell mechanism:** phason shift breaks innermost shell multiplicity from 10 → 5
- **Edge shifts → 2/5** in the large-s limit (multiplicity 4/10), exact to 10 decimal places
- **Vertex density scales as φ²** under deflation (self-similarity confirmed)
- **ratio × φ × 24 ≈ 24** — periodic and quasiperiodic denominators are dual

### The Rose Curve

The direction-dependent ratio, plotted as a polar curve, forms a **10-petaled rose** — the same shape as:
- A quasicrystal electron diffraction pattern (Shechtman, 1982)
- The mathematical rose curve r = |cos(5θ)|
- The unsmoothed form of what becomes a circle under Gaussian averaging

### The Thesis

- **Periodic world:** π, circle, 1/24, Dedekind eta, S₄, void-zero
- **Quasiperiodic world:** φ, rose, 1/φ, singular continuous spectrum, A₅, śūnya-zero
- **The Gaussian bell curve is the shape of the erasure** — it collapses the rose into a circle
- **π is what you get after smoothing** — the circumference of the averaged shape
- **James Watkins doesn't use π** because the unsmoothed shape has petals, not circumference
- **ω generates the rose** (Penrose subdivision = 0·ω = 1 as algorithm)
