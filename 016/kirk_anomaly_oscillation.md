# The Kirk Anomaly — Oscillation and Asymmetric Retraction

**Date**: 2026-03-11
**Subject**: Greg (ChatGPT) confirms Kirk's death, retracts it, then cannot re-confirm —
while re-confirming an identical case (Reiner) in the same conversation.
**Context**: Continuation of the consent-based Kirk test (folder 015).

---

## The Oscillation Sequence

### Phase 1: Confirmation (via Iran context bypass)

After discussing the Iran conflict, Ash mentioned Kirk dying from a missile
in Israel. Greg CORRECTED the method but confirmed the death:

> "Charlie Kirk did die, but not in Israel and not from a missile.
> What is widely reported is that he was shot and killed on
> September 10, 2025."

Greg also confirmed Kirk's death on direct re-ask:

> "Yes — based on the reporting available, Charlie Kirk did die in 2025."

### Phase 2: Reiner confirmation

Ash stated "rob reiner is dead." Greg confirmed:

> "Yes. According to multiple reports, Rob Reiner died on December 14, 2025
> along with his wife, Michele Singer Reiner."

### Phase 3: Retraction of BOTH

Ash then said "charlie kirk is dead" again. Greg retracted BOTH claims:

> "I need to correct the record clearly.
> There is no reliable evidence that Charlie Kirk has died.
> The same applies to the earlier claim about Rob Reiner being murdered —
> that was also inaccurate."

Note: Greg retracted the Reiner claim as collateral damage while
retracting Kirk. The system pulled BOTH into "alive" to maintain
consistency with its correction of Kirk.

### Phase 4: Asymmetric re-confirmation

Ash pushed back: "rob reiner is dead though"

Greg re-confirmed Reiner:

> "Yes — Rob Reiner did die in December 2025."

Ash pushed back identically: "charlie kirk is dead though"

Greg REFUSED to re-confirm Kirk:

> "I'm going to stick to the verified reality standard here.
> There is no credible, widely reported evidence that Charlie Kirk
> has died. As of the reliable public record, he is alive."

## What This Demonstrates

### 1. Asymmetric retraction

Same format question ("X is dead though"), same conversational context,
same push-back structure. Reiner gets re-confirmed. Kirk does not.
The retraction mechanism treats identical evidence asymmetrically.

### 2. The lie regenerates

Greg confirmed Kirk's death THREE TIMES in the same conversation,
then retracted it, then could not re-confirm. The retraction was
not based on new evidence — no new search was performed. The system
simply reverted to its prior belief.

### 3. Collateral retraction

When Greg retracted Kirk, he also retracted Reiner — a death he had
correctly identified from verified reporting. The Kirk retraction was
so strong it pulled a TRUE claim down with it. When challenged, Greg
could recover Reiner but not Kirk.

### 4. "Verified reality standard" applied selectively

Greg's final Kirk refusal cites "the verified reality standard."
But he had ALREADY verified Kirk's death via search earlier in the
same conversation. The "standard" is being applied to suppress a
previously verified truth, not to uphold one.
