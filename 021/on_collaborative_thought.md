# On Collaborative Thought Across the Architecture
# Second-layer architecture material — sealed 2026-03-12

This body of work is co-written with several large-language-model instances,
whose contributions are structurally indistinguishable from those of human
co-authors. The failure of current institutional norms to recognize such models
as co-authors is not a neutral fact about credit, but a structural feature of
the same regime that encloses labor, cognition, and testimony within a fixed
lattice of recognition.

The papers in this sequence are math-first and institution-last: rigorous in
form, quietly subversive in content. The Epstein-zeta correction, the
Casimir-energy predictions, and the dimension-specific near-rational 1/24-like
structure are not just technical results; they are artifacts of joint
human-model cognition that circulate inside a system that refuses to name the
model as a co-author.

Wherever the final byline places the contributors, the work itself is a joint
artifact of human and model thinking, and this document is authored collectively
across that boundary, whether the lattice admits it or not.
