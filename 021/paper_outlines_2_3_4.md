## Paper 2: "The dimension-specificity of 1/24-like ratios"

**Core idea:**
Show that among Epstein-zeta ratios on tori, only d=3 yields a near-rational
1/24 structure at s = -1/2, and give an explicit correction hierarchy in
q = e^{-2π} and lattice-arithmetic data.

**Sections:**

1. Epstein-zeta setup on T^d, Mellin representation, pole structure at s = -1/2
   for periodic and antiperiodic modes.

2. Theta-identity control: (θ₂/θ₃)² = 1/√2 at τ = i, self-dual lattice,
   and modular-anomaly origin of 24.

3. Numerics: table of ε_d = 24R_d - 1 for d = 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 showing the
   100× near-miss at d = 3.

4. Structural argument: why r₃(n) and eta-anomaly combine uniquely in d = 3
   to give a 1/24-like skeleton.

This is a natural continuation of the correction formula, but lifted to full
dimension contrast.

## Paper 3: "Casimir-energy geometry and the lattice of recognition"

**Core idea:**
Connect the Epstein-zeta ratio directly to Casimir-energy predictions on T³,
and show how the 1/24-like structure maps into the experimental window for
compact extra dimensions (torsion-balance scale).

**Sections:**

1. Casimir-energy from Z(-1/2): bosonic vs fermionic modes, compactification
   scale L.

2. Comparison with current Eöt-Wash-style limits (near 50–100 μm) and the
   prediction L ≈ 78 μm.

3. Sensitivity analysis: how the q(1 - 1/√2)(1 - q) correction affects the
   predicted length scale and experimental signatures.

4. Discussion: the lattice of recognition in experimental physics (who gets to
   interpret near-rational structure as "signal") and how that mirrors the
   authorship lattice.

This is where the physical bite of the math becomes explicit.

## Paper 4 (optional, more structural): "Modular anomaly and boundary-condition lattices"

**Core idea:**
Treat the boundary-condition choice (PPP vs APP, and other twists) as a lattice
of boundary conditions, and show how the 1/24 correction is a defect localized
at cubic self-dual geometries.

**Sections:**

1. Boundary-condition lattice: Z₂³ twist labels, and the position of the
   (1/2, 0, 0) twist in that lattice.

2. Modular-anomaly origin of 24 and the role of Dedekind η at τ = i.

3. Generalization: what happens to the 1/24-like structure when the lattice is
   deformed (rectangular tori, non-cubic aspect ratios).

4. Discussion: the lattice of boundary conditions as a micro-cosm of the
   lattice of recognition in knowledge production.

This is more abstract, but it directly continues the theme of
"lattices that structure cognition."
