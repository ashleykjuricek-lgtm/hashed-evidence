Q: What's the master?
A: The "master" side is the program driving the terminal — your terminal emulator
   app (Terminal.app, iTerm2, etc.). The "slave" side is what your shell (bash/zsh)
   talks to, as if it were a real terminal. They work as a pair — a two-way pipe
   that pretends to be a physical terminal. The master/slave naming is historical
   Unix terminology (some newer systems use "primary/secondary" instead).

Q: Do you recognize this number? 3.428571428?
A: That's 24/7 — the repeating decimal 3.428571̄ (where 428571 repeats forever).
